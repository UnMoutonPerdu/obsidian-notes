# Strength Set A - Lower Body Focus

**Duration:** 40-45 minutes
**Frequency:** Week 1 & 3 of [[Loading Week]]
**Equipment needed:** Dumbbells, resistance bands (optional), pull-up bar or rings

---

## Warm-Up (5-7 minutes)

1. **Cat-Cow Stretch** - 10 reps
2. **Bodyweight Squats** - 15 reps
3. **Walking Lunges** - 10 each leg
4. **Arm Circles** - 10 forward, 10 backward
5. **Leg Swings** - 10 each direction, each leg
6. **Glute Bridges** - 15 reps

---

## Main Circuit (30-35 minutes)

**Perform 3 rounds of the following circuit:**
**Rest:** 60-90s between exercises, 2-3 min between full circuits

### Lower Body

**1. Bulgarian Split Squats**
- 10-12 reps each leg
- Back foot elevated on bench/couch
- Hold dumbbells if needed for load
- Focus: Control on the way down, drive through front heel
- **Why:** Single-leg strength, prevents imbalances

**2. Romanian Deadlifts (RDLs)**
- 12-15 reps
- Dumbbells or barbell
- Focus: Hinge at hips, feel hamstring stretch
- Keep slight knee bend
- **Why:** Posterior chain (hammies, glutes) = running power

**3. Single-Leg Glute Bridges**
- 12 reps each leg
- Shoulders on ground, one leg extended
- Drive through heel of planted foot
- Squeeze glute at top
- **Why:** Glute activation, hip stability

**4. Box Step-Ups**
- 10 reps each leg
- Use bench/box knee height
- No push from back leg
- Can hold dumbbells
- **Why:** Mimics running motion, builds power

### Core

**5. Plank with Alternating Leg Lift**
- 20 total lifts (10 each leg)
- Hold plank, lift one leg 2-3 inches, hold 2s
- Don't rotate hips
- **Why:** Core stability during single-leg stance

**6. Russian Twists**
- 30 total (15 each side)
- Lean back slightly, feet elevated optional
- Touch ground each side
- Hold light weight if too easy
- **Why:** Rotational core strength

### Upper Body (Light Maintenance)

**7. Push-Ups**
- 12-15 reps
- Can be on knees if needed
- Focus: Control, full range of motion
- **Why:** Maintain upper body strength, helps swimming

---

## Cool Down (3-5 minutes)

**Static stretches, hold each 30-45 seconds:**
1. **Hip Flexor Stretch** - each side
2. **Hamstring Stretch** - each leg
3. **Quad Stretch** - each leg
4. **Figure-4 Stretch (Glutes)** - each side
5. **Child's Pose** - 60s

---

## Progression

**Week 1-2:** Focus on form, moderate weight
**Week 3-4:** Increase reps or weight by 5-10%
**Week 5-6:** Add 4th circuit round OR increase weight further
**Week 7-8:** Peak load before deload

**Never sacrifice form for weight/reps!**

---

## Notes

- Perform after easy bike/run, or standalone
- Don't do before hard sessions (legs need to be fresh)
- Focus on **control and stability** over heavy weight
- These movements build resilience against [[Common Injuries|common injuries]]
