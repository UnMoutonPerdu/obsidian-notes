# Strength Set B - Upper Body & Core Focus

**Duration:** 40-45 minutes
**Frequency:** Week 2 & 4 of [[Loading Week]]
**Equipment needed:** Dumbbells, pull-up bar/rings, resistance bands

---

## Warm-Up (5-7 minutes)

1. **Arm Circles** - 15 forward, 15 backward
2. **Scapular Shrugs** - 15 reps
3. **Band Pull-Aparts** - 15 reps
4. **Cat-Cow Stretch** - 10 reps
5. **Downward Dog to Cobra** - 8 reps
6. **Bodyweight Squats** - 15 reps (keep lower body warm)

---

## Main Circuit (30-35 minutes)

**Perform 3 rounds of the following circuit:**
**Rest:** 60s between exercises, 2 min between circuits

### Upper Body (Swimming Focus)

**1. Pull-Ups or Assisted Pull-Ups**
- 6-10 reps (adjust based on ability)
- Use band for assistance if needed
- OR: Inverted rows if no pull-up ability
- Focus: Full range, control down
- **Why:** Swimming power, lat strength

**2. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row**
- 12 reps each arm
- Bench support or hinged stance
- Pull to hip, elbow stays close to body
- Focus: Squeeze shoulder blade back
- **Why:** Swimming pull phase, back strength

**3. Dumbbell Shoulder Press**
- 12-15 reps
- Seated or standing
- Press overhead, control down
- Don't arch back excessively
- **Why:** Shoulder stability, injury prevention

**4. Band External Rotations**
- 15 reps each arm
- Elbow at 90Â°, tight to side
- Rotate forearm outward against band
- Light resistance, focus on control
- **Why:** Rotator cuff strength = [[Common Injuries|shoulder injury prevention]]

**5. Push-Up to T (or regular push-up)**
- 10-12 reps
- Push up, rotate to side plank position
- Alternate sides
- OR: standard push-ups if rotation too difficult
- **Why:** Chest, shoulder, core rotation

### Core (Bike/Run Stability)

**6. Dead Bug**
- 20 total (10 each side)
- Lie on back, opposite arm and leg extend
- Keep low back pressed to floor
- Slow, controlled movement
- **Why:** Core stability without flexion/extension

**7. Side Plank with Hip Dips**
- 10 dips each side
- Hold side plank, lower hip to ground and raise
- Can be on knee if full side plank too hard
- **Why:** Lateral core stability for running

**8. Bird Dog**
- 20 total (10 each side)
- On hands and knees, extend opposite arm and leg
- Hold 2-3s, don't rotate torso
- **Why:** Back stability, anti-rotation strength

### Lower Body (Maintenance)

**9. Walking Lunges**
- 20 total steps (10 each leg)
- Can hold light dumbbells
- Focus: Vertical torso, knee tracking over toe
- **Why:** Maintain single-leg strength

---

## Cool Down (3-5 minutes)

**Static stretches, hold each 30-45 seconds:**
1. **Doorway Chest Stretch** - 60s
2. **Shoulder Cross-Body Stretch** - each arm
3. **Tricep Overhead Stretch** - each arm
4. **Child's Pose with Lat Stretch** - each side
5. **Cobra Stretch** - 45s
6. **Thread the Needle** - each side

---

## Progression

**Week 1-2:** Learn movements, moderate intensity
**Week 3-4:** Increase reps or resistance by 5-10%
**Week 5-6:** Add 4th round OR increase load
**Week 7-8:** Peak before deload

**Rotator cuff exercises: NEVER go heavy. Control is the goal.**

---

## Notes

- Ideal after swim session (muscles already activated)
- Can do after easy run/bike
- Don't do day before hard bike or swim session
- Upper body work = swimming injury prevention
- Core work = better bike position and run economy
